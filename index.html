Why Buyers Pay

People are not paying for AI access. They are paying to avoid wasted effort and get to the few paths that actually matter.

The edge is not “we used Gemini.” The edge is removing junk opportunities, catching structural eligibility issues early, and turning raw research sprawl into a report someone can act on.

01

Fit beats volume

The goal is not a bloated list. It is a narrow set of opportunities that actually match mechanism, stage, geography, and applicant type.

02

Dead ends get removed

Weak-fit, stale, or structurally unusable funding paths are called out early so buyers do not burn hours chasing the wrong thing.

03

Judgment beats raw search

Large pools of money can still be worthless for a specific topic. The real value is knowing why something that looks promising should be excluded.

04

Action comes faster

The report is organized for decisions: what is urgent, what needs a partner, what is worth preparing, and what should be ignored.

Real example from the completed sample report: A visible Texas funding pool worth over $1.5B was excluded because it was legally structured for treatment delivery rather than preclinical R&D, while CPRIT was flagged as a strong path only if a Texas academic institution led the application.
